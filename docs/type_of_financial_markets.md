# A Ranked Taxonomy of Events That Move Financial and Betting Markets

**TL;DR**
- The single most powerful market-moving events are **systemic black swans** (pandemics, full-blown banking crises, great-power wars) — these are the only events that simultaneously move equities, credit, FX, commodities, volatility, and crypto by double-digit percentages in days. **Monetary-policy surprises (FOMC) and major tariff/trade-war shocks** are the next tier and the most actionable for traders because they happen on a known calendar but still produce 1–10% moves on surprise.
- Below those sit **inflation/CPI and payrolls (NFP) releases**, which the BIS documents as the single largest predictable volatility events in FX (FX vol rises 400–600% in the 15 minutes after release per BIS-cited research), followed by **elections, geopolitical/terror shocks, OPEC supply decisions, sovereign-debt events, and idiosyncratic corporate disasters** (frauds, mega-bankruptcies). The ranking depends critically on **surprise**: a fully-priced event moves nothing, while a moderate surprise in CPI or Fed guidance can move markets more than a war that was already feared.
- For the project's fastest-reacting markets — **forex, VIX, sovereign CDS, prediction markets, and crypto** — the highest-information events are FOMC decisions, CPI/NFP surprises, election-night results, tariff/sanction announcements, and acute geopolitical flashpoints. Prediction markets (Polymarket/Kalshi) are themselves moved primarily by *political* events (elections, scandals, military escalations) and react in seconds, but academic evidence (Clinton & Huang, Vanderbilt 2025; Bürgi, Deng & Whelan, CESifo WP 12122, 2025) shows they exhibit a favorite-longshot bias and reflect within-market dynamics as much as fundamental news.

## Key Findings

1. **"Impact" has three dimensions that must be ranked jointly:** (a) *magnitude* of the price move per unit of surprise, (b) *breadth* across asset classes, and (c) *speed* (seconds vs. days). A Fed surprise has high magnitude/breadth/speed; a hurricane has high magnitude but narrow breadth (insurance, cat-bond, energy, weather derivs); an earnings miss has high magnitude but single-name breadth.
2. **The 2008 GFC, COVID-19, and the 2022 Russia–Ukraine outbreak are the three benchmark "everything-moves" events** of the modern era. The S&P 500 fell 34% in 5 weeks in Feb–Mar 2020, the VIX hit a closing high of 82.7 (Cboe), and the Dow's three biggest single-day point drops in history all clustered in March 2020.
3. **Pre-announced central-bank decisions remain the largest *predictable* event risk.** The canonical Bernanke–Kuttner estimate is that an unexpected **25 bp Fed tightening shock causes roughly a 1% S&P 500 decline** in a tight intraday window (multiplier ≈ −4.68, t=3.03; Bernanke & Kuttner 2005, replicated in Federal Reserve FEDS Notes 2026). Forward-guidance language now matters as much as the rate itself (Swanson 2023, FRBSF WP 2025-30). Lucca & Moench (2015, *Journal of Finance* 70(1):29–71) document the pre-FOMC announcement drift: *"a staggering 80% of the annual U.S. equity premium since 1994 was earned in the 24 hours before FOMC announcements."*
4. **CPI surprises have become unusually powerful since 2021.** A hot September 2022 core CPI print drove the S&P 500 down 4.3% in one day and pushed the VIX up 14.2 points (Cboe). The *Applied Economics Letters* (2026) study "Asymmetric S&P 500 reactions to CPI surprises in a high-inflation environment" (DOI 10.1080/13504851.2026.2624038) reports: *"Positive surprises consistently generated abnormal returns of around 1% over short windows, highly significant both statistically and economically. Negative surprises, while associated with negative returns, did not reach statistical significance."*
5. **Trade-war/tariff shocks now rival monetary policy.** Trump's April 2, 2025 "Liberation Day" tariffs produced the **largest two-day S&P 500 loss in history (~10.5%), a VIX close of 45.31, and ~$6.6 trillion of market-cap destruction** — the third-biggest VIX spike ever, behind only COVID-19 and the GFC.
6. **Election shocks can be the largest single FX events.** The 2016 Brexit vote produced the **largest one-day GBP move since the collapse of Bretton Woods** (≈ −11% vs USD intraday); overnight ranges varied from −4.3% (PLN) to −11.1% (JPY). The 2016 US election sent E-mini S&P 500 futures down >6% overnight before reversing the same day. By contrast, ordinary US presidential elections move the index only ~1–4% with significant sector dispersion.
7. **9/11 produced a one-day S&P drop of 4.93% on reopening and an 11.6% weekly decline** (BIS Quarterly Review, Dec 2001), but research by Coleman (2012) and Baumbert/Buesa/Lynch (2013) finds market reactions to terror have *desensitized* over time — most post-9/11 attacks are absorbed within ~1 hour.
8. **OPEC and energy supply shocks are the largest commodity-specific events.** The October 2022 OPEC+ 2 mb/d cut, the 2023 Russia/Saudi extensions, and the February 2022 Russia invasion (Brent rising from ~$95 to >$115 within days) are the modern reference points. Iran/Strait of Hormuz scares can move Brent +16% intraday but typically fade unless physical flows are cut.
9. **Corporate events compress huge moves into single names.** Average 3-day target CAR in M&A is **+16.0%** with an average premium near **30–32%**, while acquirer CAR is **−0.7%** overall, **−1.5% in stock-financed deals, and +0.4% in cash deals** (Andrade, Mitchell & Stafford 2001, *JEP* 15:103–120; replicated in Mitchell, Pulvino & Stafford 2004, *JoF*). Negative earnings surprises produce an average ~3.5% post-announcement drop (FactSet Q3 2022 study). Fraud/scandal disclosures produce the largest single-day single-name moves on record (**Wirecard −62% on June 18, 2020 after EY could not confirm €1.9 bn in cash; FTT token −78% in 24 hours November 7–8, 2022; Lehman −94% on its Sept 15, 2008 Chapter 11 filing**).
10. **Niche markets each have their own dominant catalysts:** weather derivatives (~$25 bn climate-risk-transfer notional in 2024 per Stephen Doherty of Speedwell Climate via CME OpenMarkets; CME weather volumes up 260% in 2023 vs 2022) are driven by HDD/CDD anomalies; EU ETS carbon (auction revenue €38.8 bn in 2024 per European Commission) can move 20–25% on policy reform speculation (e.g., EUA price fell from ~€100 to €79 after the May 18, 2022 REPowerEU MSR auction proposal per Sandbag; EUA Dec contract fell ~25% in February 2026 on speculation of Linear Reduction Factor cuts per ING); freight (Baltic Dry, FBX) jumped 130%+ on Red Sea Houthi attacks from Dec 2023–Mar 2024 (OECD/ITF 2024).

## Details: Ranked List of Event Categories (Most → Least Impactful)

### Tier 1 — Systemic / Black Swan Events (largest, broadest, multi-week-to-multi-year impact)

**1. Global pandemics & health emergencies.**
*Why it dominates:* Forces simultaneous demand collapse, supply-chain shutdown, and emergency central-bank/fiscal response. *Magnitude/Speed:* S&P 500 −34% in 23 trading days (Feb 19 – Mar 23, 2020), VIX peak 82.7 (Cboe), Brent crude briefly went *negative* in April 2020, USD swap lines required globally. *Markets most affected:* All — equities, VIX, FX (USD haven bid), bonds (US 10Y to 0.71%), credit/CDS (IG spreads from ~50 to 370 bp), crypto (BTC −50% then +700%), commodities, real estate, freight, insurance/reinsurance, art. *Speed:* Days to weeks. *Relevance:* Existential to every market class in the project's scope.

**2. Major financial / banking-system crises.**
*Why:* Endogenous loss of confidence freezes funding markets and breaks valuation models. *Reference events:* 2008 GFC (Lehman bankruptcy Sept 15, 2008: ~$400 bn of CDS notional referenced Lehman per ISDA; LIBOR–OIS spread to ~360 bp; S&P 500 −6.7% on Aug 8, 2011 US downgrade with VIX +16.5 to 69.65); SVB/Signature/First Republic March 2023 (US financials sector −4% on event day per Yousaf & Goodell 2023, regional bank index −20% in a week, stablecoin USDC depegged briefly to $0.87). *Affected:* Credit/CDS dominate, then equities, FX (DXY squeeze), bonds (curve dislocations), crypto, real estate, private markets, prediction markets. *Speed:* Hours-to-days flashpoints inside a multi-week unfolding.

**3. Major interstate wars / great-power conflict outbreaks.**
*Why:* Reprice commodities, defense, sovereign credit, and global risk premia. *Reference:* Russia's Feb 24, 2022 invasion sent Brent above $100, then $115 within days (EIA); European gas (TTF) +60%+; wheat +50%; Russian sovereign CDS pricing >70% default probability; RUB −40% before capital controls. *Affected:* Commodities, FX (RUB, EUR), energy/power, sovereign CDS, equities (especially Europe), defense stocks (+30%+), shipping/freight, reinsurance. *Speed:* Hours to days; multi-month persistence.

### Tier 2 — Top-Tier Policy & Macro Catalysts

**4. Central-bank policy decisions and forward guidance (Fed > ECB > BoJ > BoE).**
*Why:* Sets the global risk-free rate that prices every asset. *Magnitude:* Bernanke–Kuttner (2005): 25 bp Fed funds surprise → ~1% S&P 500 move in a 30-minute window (multiplier −4.68, t=3.03). Brusa, Savor & Wilson show Fed announcements uniquely move *global* equities. Modern episodes: Powell's August 2022 Jackson Hole hawkish pivot caused a 3.4% S&P decline; the unexpected 50bp cut on July 6, 1995 lifted equities 4%. Lucca & Moench (2015, *JoF* 70(1)) document that "80% of the annual U.S. equity premium since 1994 was earned in the 24 hours before FOMC announcements." *Affected:* All markets, but most intensely bonds, FX, equities, equity vol, EM debt, crypto (pre-2024), gold. *Speed:* Seconds-to-minutes around the 2:00pm ET release.

**5. Major tariff/trade-war announcements & sanctions.**
*Why:* Reprices global supply chains, corporate margins, and sovereign trade balances. *Magnitude:* April 2025 "Liberation Day" tariffs caused S&P 500 −4.84% then −5.97% on consecutive days, Nasdaq into bear market, VIX +23.8 to 45.31, ~$6.6 trillion lost in two days, 10-year yields spiked from 3.86% to 4.5% (uncharacteristically, with stocks down) per St. Louis Fed and Investopedia. *Affected:* Equities (especially small-caps, trade-exposed sectors), FX (CNY, MXN, CAD), commodities, freight, EM debt, prediction markets, credit. *Speed:* Minutes (futures); days for full repricing.

**6. Sovereign-debt crises and defaults.**
*Why:* Repricing of risk-free benchmarks cascades to corporate credit and FX. *Reference:* Greek crisis (10Y yield to 35%+ in 2012); Argentina serial defaults (CDS spreads >5000 bp); US S&P downgrade Aug 5, 2011 → S&P 500 −6.7% on the next trading day, VIX +16.5 points (Cboe). CDS-bond basis can flip from near-zero to deeply positive in days (Chaumont 2022; Pelizzon et al. 2016 find a 10% change in Italian sovereign CDS spread moves bond yields 13%). *Affected:* Sovereign CDS, EM FX, EM/peripheral bonds, global equities, gold. *Speed:* Days; episodes evolve over weeks.

### Tier 3 — Scheduled Macro Data Releases

**7. CPI / inflation surprises.**
*Why:* Most direct read on central-bank path. *Magnitude:* Sept 13, 2022 core CPI surprise (0.6 vs 0.3 expected) → S&P −4.3%, VIX +14.2 to 37.2 (Cboe). *Applied Economics Letters* (2026) study finds *"positive surprises consistently generated abnormal returns of around 1% over short windows, highly significant both statistically and economically. Negative surprises, while associated with negative returns, did not reach statistical significance."* JPMorgan's published February 2026 scenario grid maps 5-bp core CPI bins to 0.5%–2.5% S&P moves. *Affected:* Equities, bonds (largest moves at 2- and 5-year tenors), USD, gold, crypto (historically), prediction markets on Fed actions. *Speed:* Milliseconds via algorithmic traders; hours of follow-through.

**8. Non-farm payrolls (NFP) and labor data.**
*Why:* Second pillar of Fed reaction function. *Magnitude:* Per BIS-cited research relayed by Plus500, **FX volatility rises 400–600% in the 15 minutes following NFP release**. JPMorgan trading desk (March 6, 2026 preview) maps NFP bins to ±1.5% S&P moves. *Affected:* USD pairs (EUR/USD, USD/JPY especially), Treasuries (2Y/5Y), gold, equity futures, crypto. *Speed:* Seconds (released 8:30am ET).

**9. Other macro data (GDP, PMI, retail sales, PCE, jobless claims).**
*Why:* Confirm or contradict NFP/CPI narrative. *Magnitude:* Smaller than CPI/NFP, typically 0.3–1% on equities for large surprises. The Jan 27, 2021 Q4 GDP miss caused S&P −3.3% and VIX +14.2 (Cboe). *Affected:* Same as CPI/NFP but muted. *Speed:* Seconds-to-minutes.

### Tier 4 — Political & Geopolitical Events

**10. National elections (especially binary/surprise outcomes).**
*Why:* Resets fiscal, regulatory, and trade expectations across all sectors. *Magnitude:* **Brexit (Jun 23, 2016): GBP −11% vs USD overnight, the largest one-day move in any of the world's four major currencies since the collapse of Bretton Woods (Costa et al. 2019, replicated in *Journal of International Economics* 2024); FTSE financials −10.8%**. US 2016: E-mini futures down >6% overnight, fully reversed by close (CME Group); cumulative 2016 sector dispersion 19.3% (S&P Global). US 2024: prediction markets called Trump win with ~58% confidence vs polls' coin-flip. Ordinary elections rarely move S&P more than 1–3% on the day. *Affected:* FX (most), equities (sector rotation), bonds, sovereign CDS, prediction markets (where they ARE the event), crypto. *Speed:* Overnight on results, days of repricing.

**11. Geopolitical conflict escalations (short of major war) & terrorism.**
*Why:* Sudden risk-off and commodity supply premium. *Magnitude:* 9/11: S&P −4.93% on reopening day, −11.6% over the week; MSCI World −12%; ~$3 trillion of global market-cap loss (BIS Dec 2001). Iran/Middle East escalations (Feb 2026): Brent +16% intraday to ~$108. Madrid 2004: MSCI −1.72%. Boston Marathon bombing, Paris attacks, etc. produced muted reactions confined to local markets. **Markets have desensitized to terrorism since 9/11** (Coleman 2012; Baumbert/Buesa/Lynch 2013). *Affected:* Oil, gold, defense stocks, airline/insurance stocks, regional FX, VIX, sovereign CDS. *Speed:* Minutes for known-source events; hours-days for ambiguous ones.

**12. Coups, regime change, sanctions packages, capital controls.**
*Why:* Reprices the affected sovereign and adjacent EMs. *Reference:* Turkish lira crises (TRY −15%+ on Erdogan rate-cut directives); Russian sanctions waves 2022 (RUB briefly to 120/USD); various EM coups (Niger, Myanmar) drove local equity halts. *Affected:* EM FX, EM equities, sovereign CDS, regional commodity exporters. *Speed:* Minutes-hours for FX; days for equities.

### Tier 5 — Commodity, Energy & Climate Shocks

**13. OPEC+ supply decisions and energy-supply disruptions.**
*Why:* Largest single mover of energy/power, freight, inflation expectations. *Magnitude:* October 2022 OPEC+ 2 mb/d cut: Brent +8% on the week (Reuters). April 2020 9.7 mb/d cut: stabilized oil after WTI went negative. Saudi unilateral cuts (June 2023): +$1–3/bbl. Strait of Hormuz / Red Sea events: Brent +5–16% intraday. *Affected:* Crude, refined products, natural gas, gasoline, energy equities, airline stocks (inverse), inflation breakevens, EM oil-exporter FX/CDS. *Speed:* Minutes on announcement.

**14. Natural disasters (hurricanes, earthquakes, floods, droughts).**
*Why:* Massive but localized losses transferred via insurance/reinsurance and cat bonds. *Magnitude:* Hurricane Katrina: exposed P&C insurers −1.55% (CAR [0,+1]), unexposed −0.98% (Gangopadhyay et al. 2010); $100 bn+ insured losses; created the modern cat-bond market (today $55 bn outstanding vs ~$4 bn in 2005, per Aon/Artemis). Hagendorff et al. (2015): average insurer −0.28% CAR per catastrophe. *Affected:* Reinsurance equities (Munich Re, Swiss Re, RNR), cat bonds, weather derivatives (HDD/CDD), agricultural commodities, regional real estate, energy (Gulf refining). *Speed:* Hours for stocks, weeks for cat-bond settlement.

**15. Extreme weather and climate-policy announcements.**
*Why:* Drives the small but growing weather-derivatives (~$25 bn notional 2024 per Speedwell Climate/CME) and carbon markets. *Magnitude:* EU ETS carbon prices fell from ~€100 to €79 (≈ −21%) after the May 18, 2022 REPowerEU announcement to auction MSR allowances (Sandbag); EUA Dec contract fell ~25% from above €92/t to below €70/t in February 2026 on speculation of Linear Reduction Factor reform (ING analysis); EU ETS auction revenue €38.8 bn in 2024 (European Commission Carbon Market Report 2025). *Affected:* Carbon (EUAs, CCAs, RGGI), weather derivs, utility equities, power markets. *Speed:* Minutes for carbon; weeks for weather contracts.

### Tier 6 — Corporate / Idiosyncratic Events

**16. Major corporate scandals, frauds, and mega-bankruptcies.**
*Why:* Largest single-name moves on record and potential systemic spillovers. *Magnitude:* Lehman −94% on Chapter 11 filing Sept 15, 2008; **Wirecard −62% on June 18, 2020** (FT) on fraud disclosure (€1.9 bn missing) — cumulative −85% before insolvency filing June 25; **FTX's FTT token −78% in 24 hours Nov 7–8, 2022** (Decrypt/CNBC), cumulative −90% within a week; Enron from $90 (Aug 2000) to $0.26 (Nov 2001) → −99%+ in 16 months, with discrete -20% to -23% single-day jumps on Oct 16 and Nov 20, 2001; SVB −60% on March 9, 2023 before being shut March 10. *Affected:* The individual stock, sector peers (contagion), CDS spreads (sharp jumps on systemic firms), prediction markets on government action. *Speed:* Minutes to hours; sector spillover days.

**17. Earnings releases.**
*Why:* Information-dense, occurs ~90 days per stock per year. *Magnitude:* Aït-Sahalia et al. (forthcoming *JFE*, working paper arXiv:2601.08962) find earnings cause jumps in stock prices in >90% of after-hours announcement sessions vs <3% in non-announcement sessions, using 89 billion after-hours quotes. FactSet Q3 2022 study: positive-surprise stocks +2.4% on average in the announcement window; negative-surprise stocks −3.5% on average (UCLA Anderson Review). Mega-caps (NVDA, AAPL) can swing the entire S&P 500 by 1%+ on earnings days. *Affected:* Single stock, sector ETFs, sometimes broad indices via mega-caps; options vol crush. *Speed:* Milliseconds via algos.

**18. M&A announcements.**
*Why:* Reprices target to deal value and acquirer to deal-quality signal. *Magnitude (Andrade, Mitchell & Stafford 2001, JEP 15:103–120, sample 1973–1998, n>4,300):* Target three-day CAR **+16.0%**; combined CAR +1.8%; acquirer CAR **−0.7%** overall, **−1.5% in stock-financed deals, +0.4% in cash deals**. Mitchell, Pulvino & Stafford (2004, *JoF* LIX(1)) confirm: *"acquiring companies that use their stock as the merger consideration experience announcement period abnormal returns between −2 percent and −3 percent."* Average control premium **~30%** above unaffected price (Fidelity meta-analysis; Baker et al. 2012 replication: 32%). Hostile/contested deals can produce 40–50% target moves. *Affected:* The two stocks, sector peers, M&A arbitrage funds, sometimes CDS. *Speed:* Seconds on announcement.

**19. Regulatory and legal events.**
*Why:* Reprices entire industries (FAA grounding, FDA approvals, antitrust). *Magnitude:* Boeing 737 MAX after Ethiopian Airlines crash Mar 11, 2019: down −13% intraday, closing −5.3%; cumulative ~−18% over 2 weeks (~$40 bn market cap erased — Bloomberg/CBS). FDA drug approvals routinely produce ±30–80% biotech moves. SEC spot-Bitcoin-ETF approval Jan 10, 2024: BTC eventually +160% over the year, ETFs accumulated $18.9 bn net inflow in the first 9 months (Grayscale Research). *Affected:* Sector-specific equities; FDA → biotech vol; antitrust → tech megacaps; ETF approvals → crypto. *Speed:* Seconds.

**20. Leadership changes & C-suite departures.**
*Why:* Repricing of strategy, governance, succession risk. *Magnitude:* Modest in normal cases (1–5%), but extreme on founder departures or sudden CEO deaths (Tesla, Apple in 2011, etc.). *Speed:* Minutes.

### Tier 7 — Technological, Social & Behavioral

**21. Cyber attacks, hacks, and major IT outages.**
*Why:* Direct revenue/security impact + sector-wide reputation. *Magnitude:* CrowdStrike global outage July 19, 2024: CRWD −11% on day-1, −38% over 2 weeks; SolarWinds breach Dec 2020: SWI −22% next day; Equifax 2017 breach: EFX −35% over 2 weeks. Crypto exchange hacks routinely produce 10–30% drops in affected token. *Affected:* Individual firm, cybersecurity sector (inverse hedge), crypto. *Speed:* Hours to days.

**22. Social-media / viral / meme-driven events.**
*Why:* Retail-driven flow can detach prices from fundamentals. *Magnitude:* GameStop January 2021: +1700% in 3 weeks; AMC, BBBY, "roaring kitty" episodes 2024 (+74% intraday on a single tweet). Elon Musk tweets routinely move TSLA 5–10% and BTC/DOGE 10–20%. *Affected:* Individual stocks, crypto (especially memes), short-vol, options gamma. *Speed:* Seconds-to-minutes.

**23. Strikes, labor unrest, civil protests.**
*Why:* Supply-side cost and political-risk impact. *Magnitude:* UAW strike Sept–Oct 2023: Big 3 automaker equities −5–10%, eventual contracts +25% wage; French pension protests; Hong Kong 2019 protests (HKD CDS spread out, Hang Seng −15% over months). *Affected:* Sector equities, regional FX, commodities (rail/port strikes), freight indices. *Speed:* Days.

### Tier 8 — Niche & Generally Lower Impact (but high within their own market)

**24. Freight and shipping disruptions.** Baltic Dry +6.4% Q1 2024 on Red Sea escalation; global container rates +130% Nov 2023–Mar 2024 (Drewry/OECD ITF); FBX Asia–N. Europe +14% on Houthi diversions; Strait of Hormuz closure 2026: Shanghai–Jebel Ali rates quadrupled from <$2,000 to >$8,000/FEU (Freightos). Mostly affects shipping equities, freight derivatives, energy.

**25. Carbon and emissions policy events.** EU ETS moves of 20–25% on policy reform speculation (REPowerEU May 2022; EUA collapse Feb 2026 on LRF reform talk); auction revenue €38.8 bn in 2024. Mostly contained to carbon markets and utilities.

**26. Routine credit-rating agency actions.** Significant only for sovereigns (S&P US downgrade 2011 was sui generis) or marginal IG/HY-line crossings. Corporate downgrade announcements typically move CDS spreads 20–80 bp on the day.

**27. NFT / collectibles / art events.** Highly idiosyncratic; OpenSea volume −95% from 2022 peak; Sotheby's/Christie's auction events affect specific segments. Tiny but visible in alternative-asset prediction markets.

**28. Real-estate / housing data.** Case-Shiller, NAR existing-home sales — typically <0.3% S&P reaction, but can move REITs and homebuilders 2–5% on big surprises.

## Notes on Speed, Reliance on Surprise, and Project-Specific Relevance

- **Surprise dominates magnitude.** A 25 bp rate hike fully priced moves nothing; a 25 bp hike with hawkish guidance against a dovish consensus moves the S&P 1%+. The Trade Policy Uncertainty (Caldara et al.) index and Citi Economic Surprise Index are the cleanest "surprise" measures.
- **For prediction markets (Polymarket / Kalshi):** the most predictive events are elections, geopolitical escalation, regulatory/legal milestones (court rulings, Fed decisions), specific corporate events (CEO departures), and binary economic data. Both platforms show favorite-longshot bias per Bürgi, Deng & Whelan (2025, CESifo WP 12122 / CEPR DP 20631): *"investors who buy contracts costing less than 10c lose over 60 percent of their money."* Their data also shows *"takers lose almost 32% on average, while makers have an average loss of about 10%"* on a pre-fee basis. Clinton & Huang (Vanderbilt, 2025) analyzed *"more than 2,500 political prediction markets traded across the Iowa Electronic Markets, Kalshi, PredictIt, and Polymarket during the final five weeks of the 2024 U.S. presidential campaign involving more than two billion dollars in transactions"* and found Kalshi accurate 78% of the time, Polymarket 67%, PredictIt 93%.
- **For VIX / volatility markets:** the highest-impact events are tail risks — pandemics (VIX 82.7), GFC (VIX 80.86 Oct 2008), Liberation Day tariffs (VIX 45.31 Apr 2025), Aug 2024 Yen carry unwind, 2010 Flash Crash, US debt downgrade 2011. CPI/NFP/FOMC surprises usually move VIX 2–8 points.
- **For sovereign CDS:** sovereign-debt events, US fiscal-cliff/debt-ceiling episodes, war outbreaks, and Fed/ECB liquidity decisions move spreads the most. Pelizzon et al. (2016): a 10% change in Italian sovereign CDS spread moves 13% in bond yields.
- **For crypto:** Pre-2024 crypto was a high-beta liquidity asset moved by Fed decisions. Since the Jan 2024 spot-BTC ETF approval, Binance Research (2026) documents BTC has shifted from a "macro lagging receiver" to a "leading pricer" — correlation with the Global Easing Breadth Index flipped from +0.21 to −0.78 — making ETF-flow data, regulatory milestones, and on-chain events the dominant near-term catalysts.
- **For FX:** NFP, CPI, FOMC, and election-night results are unambiguously the largest movers, with BIS-cited research showing FX vol +400–600% in 15 minutes around NFP.
- **For commodities/energy:** OPEC decisions, geopolitical conflict in producing regions, weather events, and inventory data (EIA Wednesday at 10:30am ET) dominate.

## Recommendations

For a project predicting business/market reactions to social and political events, build the system in four staged layers, with thresholds that move events between layers based on observed market moves:

1. **Tier-1/2 always-on monitoring (build first).** Continuously ingest: FOMC/ECB/BoJ statements and press conferences (with NLP for hawkish/dovish shift), CPI/PCE/NFP/GDP releases (with consensus deltas), major-power conflict news (Reuters/Bloomberg high-priority alerts), pandemic/WHO PHEIC declarations, sovereign-debt headlines (S&P/Moody's/Fitch rating actions), and tariff/sanctions announcements (USTR, EU Official Journal, OFAC). *Benchmark to escalate:* any single event projected to move S&P 500 ≥1.5%, VIX ≥+5 pts, or DXY ≥0.7%.

2. **Tier-3/4 event-driven layer (build second).** Election-night odds feeds (Polymarket/Kalshi/PredictIt APIs, AP/Decision Desk feeds), OPEC meeting calendars, FDA/SEC/FTC/DOJ docket monitoring, top-100 corporate earnings calendars with options-implied move pricing. *Benchmark:* events that historically produce ≥1% sector ETF moves.

3. **Tier-5/6 cross-asset overlay (build third).** Hurricane track / NOAA feed → reinsurance and cat-bond pricing; EIA weekly inventories → WTI/Brent; ENTSO-E and EEX → power and EUA carbon; Drewry/FBX/Baltic Dry → freight derivatives. *Benchmark:* events that produce specific-market moves ≥3 standard deviations.

4. **Tier-7/8 social/idiosyncratic layer (build last, lowest priority).** Twitter/X firehose for viral/meme events, dark-pool and unusual options flow, NFT/collectibles index moves. *Benchmark:* idiosyncratic moves only relevant for narrow strategy modules.

**Stop-rule for prediction-market signals:** if a Polymarket or Kalshi contract is at a price below $0.10 or above $0.90, treat it as having favorite-longshot bias (Bürgi, Deng & Whelan 2025) and adjust the implied probability toward the consensus of polls/Iowa Electronic Markets/PredictIt rather than taking the price at face value. If Polymarket and Kalshi diverge by >5 percentage points on the same contract, treat the higher-volume venue's price as a noisy estimate and look for the cause in within-market trading dynamics (Clinton & Huang 2025) rather than fundamental news.

**Threshold to flag a "black swan" regime:** if any of {VIX > 35; SPX 1-day move > 3% absolute; 10Y yield 1-day move > 25 bp; investment-grade CDX > 100 bp; DXY 1-day move > 1.5%; oil 1-day move > 7%} are breached, the project's normal Tier-3/4 ranking is suspended and Tier-1 systemic-event logic dominates.

## Caveats

- **Reactions are conditional, not fixed.** The same nominal event (e.g., 25 bp hike) moves markets differently depending on positioning, sentiment, and the macro regime (Andersen et al. 2007; ScienceDirect 2022 sentiment-state study on FOMC). The 2026 Fed shift toward less forward guidance (the so-called "Warsh environment") means surprises may be larger going forward.
- **Magnitudes cited are historical averages or specific events**, not forecasts. Single events with similar headlines (e.g., Brexit vs. Italian referendum) produced very different moves.
- **Many of the largest moves in history were not classified as "events" in real time** — they emerged from cascading liquidity dynamics (Oct 1987 Black Monday, May 2010 Flash Crash, Aug 2024 Yen carry unwind). Pure event-driven systems will miss these and need volatility-regime overlays.
- **Prediction-market accuracy is contested.** Bürgi, Deng & Whelan (2025) document that on Kalshi, takers lose ~32% pre-fee on average and makers lose ~10%, with a strong favorite-longshot bias. Clinton & Huang's (Vanderbilt, 2025) study of >2,500 contracts across the final five weeks of the 2024 cycle found Polymarket only 67% accurate, suggesting price ≠ probability, especially for low-information contracts.
- **The 2025 tariff regime is unprecedented in the modern era** and may not generalize backward to the 2010s; some statistical relationships (e.g., S&P-Treasury correlation flipping positive on Liberation Day, when Treasuries usually rally on stock declines) are new and not yet well-modeled.
- **Crypto's relationship to macro has structurally changed post-ETF (Jan 2024).** Backtests using pre-2024 data may not reflect current sensitivities. Binance Research's BTC–Global Easing Breadth Index correlation flipping from +0.21 to −0.78 is the most striking documented case.
- **War/terror desensitization is a real phenomenon** (Coleman 2012; Baumbert/Buesa/Lynch 2013). Each successive event of similar character produces a smaller market reaction unless materially larger in scope.
